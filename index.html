Introduction

IJMB in Nigeria is designed for students who want to secure university admission without relying solely on JAMB UTME. Through the IJMB programme, candidates can gain admission into federal, state, and private universities via Direct Entry after completing a 9–12 month academic session.

The Interim Joint Matriculation Board (IJMB) programme is one of the most trusted and recognized advanced level (A-Level) pathways for gaining Direct Entry admission into 200 level in Nigerian universities. Established and moderated by Ahmadu Bello University, IJMB is approved by the Federal Government of Nigeria and accepted by over 90% of universities nationwide.

What Is IJMB?

IJMB stands for Interim Joint Matriculation Board, an advanced level (A-Level) programme that allows students to gain Direct Entry admission into 200 level in Nigerian universities without writing JAMB UTME again.

How IJMB Works?

  • The programme runs for 9–12 months.
  • Students choose three A-Level subjects related to their intended university course.
  • At the end of the programme, candidates sit for final IJMB examinations.
  • The result is used to apply for Direct Entry (DE) admission into 200 level through JAMB.

Who Should Consider IJMB?

The following categories can consider IJMB for D.E admission:

  • Students who did not meet their desired JAMB score
  • Candidates seeking an alternative admission route
  • Students who want to avoid repeated UTME attempts
  • Those aiming for competitive courses like Medicine, Law, Engineering, or Accounting

IJMB Admission Requirements

To apply for IJMB registration in Nigeria, you typically need:

  • Minimum of five (5) O’Level credits (WAEC, NECO, or NABTEB)
  • Credits (C6 minimum) must include English Language and Mathematics (depending on course)
  • Awaiting results may also apply

IJMB Subject Combinations

Students select three A-Level subjects related to their intended university course. Examples:

  • Medicine: Biology, Chemistry, Physics
  • Engineering: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry
  • Law: Government, Literature, CRS/IRS
  • Accounting: Economics, Government, Mathematics

Choosing the correct IJMB subject combination is critical for Direct Entry eligibility.
